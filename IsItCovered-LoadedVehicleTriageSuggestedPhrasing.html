Is it Covered? Loaded Vehicle Call Handling

This reference is designed to help you quickly process loaded vehicle calls by outlining your role in eligibility decisions and providing a clear view of the service provider’s on-scene responsibilities.

📞 ERS Receive & Dispatch – Load Assessment & Documentation

Step 1: Coverage
Question

Is the vehicle covered?

Confirm active membership and eligible vehicle before discussing load specifics.
Step 2: Document Load Details
Question

What is loaded in the vehicle?
Item type, quantity, and approximate size/weight.

Action Document details clearly in Driver Notes. Capture any statements that indicate size, weight, or position.
Suggested Phrasing "To ensure we send the right equipment, could you describe the items in the vehicle and the approximate weight?"
Step 3: Manage Expectations
Action Complete the service request.

Counsel the member that the service provider will assess upon arrival. Setting clear expectations helps to reduce escalations.
Suggested Phrasing "I’ve noted the details for the driver. The technician will determine if the vehicle can be towed safely upon arrival."

🛻 Technician On-Scene Assessment Field Decision

Load Assessment
Question

Does the load contain excluded items?

Stop The provider will determine if the load contains items excluded by policy. If so, a COD referral should be offered.
Safety & Capacity
Question Is the load secured in the vehicle?
Question Is the load within weight limits?
Question Can the tow truck safely tow it?
Coverage Determination
Action If YES to all 3, tow may be completed.
Decline If NO to one or more, decline service and offer COD referral.
