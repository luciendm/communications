AAA Protected & Confidential Information Requests

ERS Call Center Guide
Incoming Information Request
Determine if the caller might be requesting protected or confidential member or Club information.
❓ Is the caller requesting protected or confidential information?
⬇️
Step 1 – Who is calling?
Potential Caller Types
  • News media / reporters
  • General public
  • Law enforcement
  • Legal representatives
  • Concerned friends or relatives
Protected Information Examples
  • Internal policies not related to roadside processing
  • Employee information
  • Member account or personal details
  • Roadside events in the media
  • Previously reported Critical Incident details
  • Roadside details unrelated to the caller’s membership
⬇️
🟢 If NO – Continue Normal Processing
  • Verify the member's identity
  • Follow ERS protocol guiding what information may be shared
  • Handle the call normally
🔴 If YES – Restricted Request

ERS Agents Must NOT:

  • Provide protected information
  • Create a CMS Case
  • Transfer to SRT or the ACE Supervisor Line

ERS Agents MUST:

  • Transfer caller to the Local Supervisor Line
  • Avoid policy explanations, personnel info, event details, or opinions
⬇️
ERS Management Team Handling
Missing Member / Household Member
  • Obtain Case Number or Account Name
  • Review documentation
  • Assume ownership
  • Notify Corporate Security
  • Email ACESecurity@ace.aaa.com
Law Enforcement / Legal Representative

Collect:

  • Caller name
  • Agency/company
  • Requested information
  • Contact info

Response: “We will forward your request for review.” Do not promise a callback or timeframe.

Action:

  • Forward to Corporate Security
  • Email ACESecurity@ace.aaa.com
If you have any questions, please reach out to your local management team.
